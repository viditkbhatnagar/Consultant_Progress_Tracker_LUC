# Children's Privacy Notice

> v0.1 — drafted 2026-04-26 · Effective date: `[FILL]` · Last reviewed: 2026-04-26 · Owner: DPO `[FILL]`

This Notice is for parents and legal guardians of students enrolled in
Skillhub Training and Skillhub Institute. It explains, in plain language,
what we do with your child's personal data on the Team Progress Tracker
platform ("the Platform").

It supplements, and should be read together with, our main
[Privacy Policy](01-privacy-policy.md).

## 1. Who we are

`[FILL: Skillhub Training and Skillhub Institute, divisions of registered
company name]`. Contact: `[FILL: privacy@…]` · DPO: `[FILL: name + email]`.

## 2. Why a separate notice for children

UAE PDPL Art. 9 and EU GDPR Art. 8 set additional rules for processing
the personal data of minors. We treat data about students under the age
of `[FILL: confirmed age threshold]` as data about a child for the
purposes of this notice and apply the additional safeguards below.

## 3. What data we collect about your child

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identity | Name, date of birth, gender, nationality |
| Contact | Phone, email, residential area / emirate, school |
| Parent / guardian contact | Mother's and father's phone and email (we do not collect for the child's other relatives by default) |
| Academic | Curriculum (IGCSE / CBSE), academic year, grade, subjects, mode (online / offline / hybrid / one-to-one) |
| Financial | Course fee, registration fee, EMI plan and payment history, outstanding amount |
| Behavioural | Counselor's free-text notes about meetings, follow-ups, and admission progress |

We do **not** intentionally collect health, biometric, or special
category data. We do **not** track location beyond the residence /
emirate field.

## 4. Why we collect it (lawful basis)

We process your child's data on the basis of your **parental consent**
(PDPL Art. 9 / GDPR Art. 8). For some processing — for example,
fulfilling the educational service you have signed up for, or meeting our
record-keeping obligations under UAE law — we additionally rely on
**performance of a contract** (with you) or **legal obligation**.

You give consent by signing the
[Parental Consent Form](08-parental-consent-form.md) at admission. You
can withdraw it at any time by contacting `[FILL: privacy@…]`. Withdrawal
does not affect the lawfulness of processing already done; it may mean
we cannot continue providing the service.

## 5. Who we share it with

We share your child's data with the four sub-processors listed in our
[Sub-processor List](06-subprocessor-list.md): MongoDB Atlas (database,
Ireland), Render (hosting, Singapore), and (in adult LUC contexts only,
not for your child today) OpenAI and Groq.

**AI services and your child's data**: until we confirm DPAs with the AI
providers and add redaction safeguards to the application, the AI chat
features available to staff do not query your child's records. See our
[Security Gap Roadmap](../security/14-security-gap-analysis-and-remediation-roadmap.md)
items SEC-05 and SEC-13 for the engineering view.

We do not sell your child's data.

## 6. Cross-border transfer

Your child's data is stored in the European Union (Ireland). It travels
through Singapore each time staff load the Platform. We rely on
`[FILL: appropriate safeguards under PDPL Art. 22-23 and GDPR Art.
44-49]` for these transfers.

## 7. How long we keep it

| Category | Retention |
|---|---|
| Active enrolment records | While enrolled + 7 years (UAE accounting record-keeping) |
| Closed enrolments / withdrawn applications | 3 years from withdrawal |
| Counselor free-text notes | Same as enrolment record |
| EMI / fee history | While enrolled + 7 years |
| Parental consent record | Same as enrolment record |

Full schedule: [Records Retention Schedule](09-records-retention-schedule.md).

## 8. Your rights as a parent / guardian

You can, on behalf of your child:

- See the data we hold (right of access).
- Correct inaccurate data.
- Ask us to delete data when it is no longer necessary or when you
  withdraw consent and no other lawful basis applies.
- Restrict or object to processing in defined cases.
- Receive an export of your child's data in a portable format.
- Complain to the UAE Data Office or to the EU lead supervisory
  authority `[FILL]`.

To exercise any right, email `[FILL: privacy@…]`. We will reply within
30 days. Where the request is complex we may extend by up to 60 days
and will tell you why.

## 9. When the child becomes an adult

When your child reaches `[FILL: age of majority]`, they can exercise the
rights above on their own. We will, at that point, write to the email
address on file (your child's, or yours if no separate address has
been provided) to confirm the change in handling.

## 10. Security

We protect your child's data with the controls described in our
[Information Security Policy](../security/01-information-security-policy.md)
and our [Data Classification & Handling](../security/03-data-classification-and-handling.md)
rules, which classify minors' data at the highest sensitivity level
("Restricted").

## 11. Honest disclosures

We believe parents are entitled to plain-language honesty. So:

- We currently store your child's data in Ireland and process it through
  Singapore. That is a four-jurisdiction footprint (UAE, Singapore,
  Ireland, occasionally the US for AI features used by staff in non-child
  contexts).
- Our written agreements with our AI vendors have **not** been confirmed
  signed at the date of this Notice (`[FILL: confirmation pending]`).
  Until they are, we keep your child's records out of the AI chat. Our
  internal record of this commitment is in
  [Security Gap Roadmap](../security/14-security-gap-analysis-and-remediation-roadmap.md)
  items SEC-05 and SEC-13.
- We do not yet enforce multi-factor authentication for staff accessing
  the Platform. We are working on this; see the same Roadmap, item
  SEC-07. In the meantime, we deactivate stale accounts and review
  active access quarterly.

You may always ask the DPO for the current status of any of the items
above.

## 12. Changes to this notice

If we change anything material in this notice — particularly anything
about how we share your child's data — we will tell you by email at
least 14 days before the change takes effect.
